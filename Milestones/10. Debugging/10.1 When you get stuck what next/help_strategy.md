## Reflection 

### When do you prefer using AI vs. searching Google?
- I prefer using AI when I’m trying to understand a new concept or when I’m stuck on a specific problem and need a quick explanation or direction. It’s especially useful for breaking things down in a simple way and giving examples I can work from. On the other hand, I use Google when I need reliable resources like official documentation, detailed articles, or to confirm information. Google is also better when I want to see how other developers have handled similar issues, especially through forums or real-world examples

### How do you decide when to ask a colleague instead?
- I usually ask a colleague when the issue involves sensitive information that I can’t share with AI or search engines, or when I’ve been stuck for a while without making progress. I also reach out when the problem is related to setup or environment configuration, as these can often be resolved much faster with guidance from someone more familiar with the system

### What challenges do developers face when troubleshooting alone?
- One of the main challenges is going down the wrong path without realising it, which can waste a lot of time. It’s easy to overthink the problem or focus on the wrong part of the code. Without another perspective, it can also be harder to validate assumptions or spot simple mistakes. This can lead to frustration, especially when the issue is something small but not immediately obvious, like a configuration error or incorrect dependency
